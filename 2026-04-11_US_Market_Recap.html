2026-04-11 US Market Recap

  1. Market Summary 📊
    The US market exhibited a polarized performance on April 11, 2026, as investors grappled with persistent inflation data against a backdrop of robust AI-driven growth. While the Nasdaq managed a modest gain of 0.35% fueled by big tech, the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 0.56% as “hot” inflation concerns weighed on traditional sectors. The S&P 500 remained nearly flat, declining 0.11% to close at 6816.89.

  2. Index Commentary 📉
    Index divergence was the defining feature of the session. The Nasdaq Composite outperformed, reclaiming momentum as AI optimism offset macro headwinds. Conversely, the Dow Jones lagged significantly, pressured by PCE and CPI data that suggested inflation remains sticky, impacting rate-sensitive blue-chip components. The S&P 500 acted as a pivot point, staying resilient despite the broader Dow weakness. The VIX retreated 1.33% to 19.23, suggesting that while indices are mixed, there is no immediate panic in the options market.

  3. Fund Flows 💸
    Capital flows indicate a clear “flight to growth” within the equity market. Investors are rotating out of value and industrial stocks (Dow) and into high-conviction AI themes (Nasdaq). Despite the “hot” macro narrative, the lack of a significant spike in the VIX suggests that selling is orderly and concentrated in sectors sensitive to higher-for-longer interest rates, rather than a systemic de-risking event.

  4. Sector/Theme Highlights 🚀
    AI & Cloud Infrastructure: This remains the dominant alpha driver. Amazon (AMZN) saw significant momentum following CEO Andy Jassy’s shareholder letter emphasizing an AI push. CoreWeave (CRWV) also captured headlines with a massive $21B AI-related valuation milestone, boosting sentiment across the semiconductor and data center ecosystem.
    Inflation-Sensitive Groups: Traditional sectors and high-yield dividend ETFs are facing headwinds as PCE inflation remains “hot,” complicating the Federal Reserve’s path and keeping Treasury yields in focus.

  5. Outlook 🔭
    The market is currently in a “bifurcated regime.” In the short term, the Nasdaq appears positioned to test higher levels if AI leadership persists. However, the broader market (S&P 500 and Dow) will likely remain range-bound or under pressure until inflation data shows a clear cooling trend. Watch for a potential convergence where either tech pulls the market up or macro gravity eventually drags tech down.

  6. Risk Alerts ⚠️
    Inflation Persistence: If upcoming CPI/PCE data continues to surprise to the upside, the “higher-for-longer” narrative will intensify, potentially triggering a valuation reset for growth stocks.
    Volatility Threshold: The VIX is hovering near the 20 level; a sustained break above this mark would signal a shift from a “buy the dip” environment to a “sell the rally” regime.

  7. Strategy Plan 📝
    Market Stance: Neutral (Selective Alpha).
    Position Sizing: Moderate (50-60% equity exposure), favoring mega-cap tech with strong cash flows.
    Strategy: Focus on “Relative Strength” within the Nasdaq 100 while reducing exposure to debt-heavy industrial or regional banking names.
    Invalidation Trigger: A breakdown of the S&P 500 below recent support levels or the VIX closing above 22 on high volume.
