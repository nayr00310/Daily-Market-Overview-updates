  1. Fund Flows 💸
    Capital flows are demonstrating an appetite for risk as the global bond selloff eased, with the 10-year Treasury yield stabilizing near 4.59%. While Bitcoin saw a slight retreat (-0.39%), the overall flow remains skewed toward equity growth factors.

  2. Sector Highlights 🚀
    The Technology sector, specifically Semiconductors, remains the primary engine of the current regime following Nvidia’s “blistering” chip sales report. Small-cap stocks showed significant relative strength, outperforming large-cap indices as investors seek “catch-up” opportunities. However, news of plunging oil prices suggests potential weakness in the Energy sector, and some “alarm bells” are ringing regarding the divergence between record-high equities and underlying bond market pressure.

  3. Outlook 🔭
    The near-term outlook is firmly bullish as the market processes the Nvidia catalyst and the symbolic breakthrough of Dow 50,000. Momentum is likely to persist in the coming sessions; however, the market is entering a phase where it may become sensitive to geopolitical developments and inflation data that could disrupt the current “soft landing” or “AI-growth” narrative.

  4. Risk Alerts ⚠️
    Bond Market Divergence: The growing gap between equity performance and bond market signals could lead to a sudden correction if yields spike again.
    Geopolitical Tension: Ongoing geopolitical turmoil (specifically mentions of Iran) remains a tail-risk for energy prices and global sentiment.
    Overextension: With indices at record levels, any disappointment in upcoming macro data could trigger rapid profit-taking.
