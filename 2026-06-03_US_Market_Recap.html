2026-06-03 US Market Recap

1. Market Summary 📈

The US market extended its record-breaking run on June 2, with major indices closing at fresh all-time highs despite a narrowing of gains. While the Dow Jones Industrial Average led the advance, the Nasdaq Composite remained nearly flat as momentum in mega-cap technology stocks like Alphabet and Microsoft showed signs of cooling. Volatility continues to subside, with the VIX dropping 1.74% to 15.77.

2. Index Commentary 📉

  • S&P 500: Reached a new record close of 7,609.78 (+0.13%), maintaining its longest win streak of the year.
  • Dow Jones Industrial Average: Outperformed the broader market with a 0.45% gain to 51,307.79, driven by strength in value and cyclical components.
  • Nasdaq Composite: Lagged significantly, rising only 0.03% to 27,093.90. The index wavered throughout the session as heavyweights Alphabet and Microsoft slid, offsetting gains in other areas.
  • VIX: The “fear gauge” fell to 15.77, indicating a lack of immediate hedging demand despite the indices trading at historical extremes.

3. Fund Flows 💸

Market participation appears to be rotating out of the “Magic Formula” stocks that dominated previous months. While the S&P 500 and Dow are extending win streaks, the divergence in the Nasdaq suggests a selective profit-taking environment in high-growth tech. Overseas, manufacturing contraction in Malaysia signals a potential cooling in global industrial demand, which may influence future capital flows into defensive sectors.

4. Sector Highlights 🛍️

  • Retail/Consumer: Victoria’s Secret was the standout performer, rocketing 47% in its best single-day gain ever following strong Q1 sales and a raised forecast.
  • Technology: The sector faced headwinds as Alphabet and Microsoft saw declines, leading to a “wavering” Nasdaq.
  • Crypto-linked Assets: Bitcoin fell below the $68,000 threshold, potentially weighing on sentiment for digital asset-related equities.

5. Outlook 🔭

The short-term outlook remains cautiously bullish as indices continue to print record highs. However, the slowing momentum in the Nasdaq and the failure of traditional “Magic Formula” strategies suggest the market is entering a transition phase. Investors should look for continued rotation into laggards (like retail) while monitoring if the Dow can sustain the market’s upward trajectory in the absence of tech leadership.

6. Risk Alerts ⚠️

  • Tech Exhaustion: Sustained sliding in Alphabet and Microsoft could eventually drag down the S&P 500.
  • Macro Contraction: Manufacturing weakness in emerging markets (e.g., Malaysia) may signal broader supply chain or demand issues.
  • Overextension: Markets are at record highs following one of the best two-month stretches in history, increasing the risk of a technical mean reversion.

7. Strategy Plan 🛡️

  • Stance: Balanced. Maintain exposure to broad indices but shift focus toward high-quality cyclicals and retail names showing earnings-driven breakouts.
  • Position-Sizing: Maintain standard core positions; avoid chasing mega-cap tech at current levels given the recent slide in Alphabet/Microsoft.
  • Invalidation Trigger: A close in the S&P 500 below recent support levels or a sharp spike in the VIX above 18.00 would signal a shift to a defensive posture.
